MATER PRIVATE NETWORK
JOB DESCRIPTION
Full Stack Developer

Job Title: Full Stack Developer
Department: IT
Reporting Line: Head of Digital Engineering &amp; Interoperability
Purpose of the Role:
The Full Stack Developer will contribute to the design, development, and support of secure, scalable
digital applications across clinical and patient-facing healthcare systems.
This role is ideal for a developer with strong core engineering foundations who is eager to grow their full
stack skills in a complex, regulated environment. Working closely with senior engineers, product teams,
and UX designers, the successful candidate will help deliver high-quality, user-centred digital solutions,
while developing their technical and professional capabilities.

Duties &amp; Responsibilities:
Software Development
• Contribute to the design, development, testing, and maintenance of full stack applications, including
frontend and backend components.
• Build and enhance RESTful APIs and backend services under guidance from senior engineers.
• Develop responsive, accessible, and performant user interfaces for web and mobile-first applications.
• Implement features based on well-defined requirements, designs, and user stories.
• Write clean, maintainable, and well-tested code following engineering best practices.
Backend &amp; Platform Development
• Develop backend services using modern frameworks and architectures, supported by senior team
members.
• Work with databases to implement data models, queries, and persistence layers.
• Assist with integration development, including API consumption and event-based messaging.
• Support production systems through bug fixing, monitoring, and incident resolution.
Frontend Engineering
•Build and maintain modern React, Angular, or Vue-based user interfaces.
• Deliver responsive and accessible UI components, aligned with UX design standards.
• Collaborate closely with UX designers and product teams to deliver intuitive user experiences.

DevOps &amp; Engineering Practices
• Contribute to CI/CD pipelines and automated testing workflows.
• Develop using containerised and cloud-native environments, with mentoring and support.
• Learn and apply DevOps and cloud engineering best practices.
• Participate in team-based incident response and system support activities.
Quality, Security &amp; Compliance
• Follow secure coding and data protection best practices.
• Contribute to automated testing and quality assurance efforts.
• Ensure adherence to GDPR and healthcare data protection standards.
Skills &amp; Qualifications:
Essential
• Degree in Computer Science, Software Engineering, or related discipline, or equivalent experience.
• 2–4 years professional software development experience.
• Practical experience in:
◦ Frontend: React, Angular, or Vue; modern JavaScript/TypeScript
◦ Backend: Node.js, Java (Spring Boot), .NET, or Python
◦ API Development: RESTful services
◦ Databases: PostgreSQL, MySQL, SQL Server, or MongoDB
◦ Version Control: Git
•Experience contributing to production-grade applications in a team environment.
Desirable Experience
• Exposure to cloud platforms (Azure, AWS, or GCP).
• Familiarity with container technologies (Docker).
• Experience in regulated or enterprise environments.
• Awareness of healthcare systems, interoperability, or clinical workflows.
Key Competencies
• Strong problem-solving mindset
• Willingness to learn and develop
• Attention to detail and quality
• Collaborative team player
• Clear technical communication

Terms and Conditions of Employment
Remuneration: As per contract
Annual Leave: As per contract
Probation: Permanent appointment is subject to a probationary period of 6 months, which if required may be
extended.
Health: An independent medical advisor appointed by the hospital will carry out a pre-employment medical.
Hours of Work: Thirty-seven hours each week exclusive of meal breaks, with flexibility (regarding actual
working hours) to suit the local work practices and demands of the service.
Confidentiality: The post holder must maintain the confidentiality of information about patients, staff and other
health service business in accordance with Hospital Policy.
Uniform: Attire must be in line with departmental policy: a neat, clean, business-like appearance must be
always maintained.
